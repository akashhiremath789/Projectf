IceCream Dessert

Ingredients

  • 4 scoops vanilla ice cream (or any flavor of choice)
  • 1 cup cornflakes, crushed
  • 1/2 cup desiccated coconut (optional)
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup cold water
  • Oil for deep frying
  • Chocolate syrup or honey, for serving
  • Whipped cream and cherries, for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Shape and freeze: Scoop the ice cream into firm balls. Place them on a tray lined with parchment paper and freeze for at least 2–3 hours, or until completely hard.
  2. Prepare coating: In a bowl, mix crushed cornflakes and desiccated coconut (if using). In another bowl, make a batter by combining all-purpose flour and cold water to form a smooth, thick paste.
  3. Coat the ice cream: Take one frozen ice cream ball at a time, dip it quickly in the flour batter, and then roll it in the cornflake mixture. Ensure it’s completely covered. Return to the freezer for 1–2 hours to firm up again.
  4. Heat oil: Heat oil in a deep pan or wok. The oil should be hot but not smoking (around 180°C / 350°F).
  5. Fry quickly: Remove the coated ice cream ball from the freezer and immediately fry for 10–15 seconds, just until golden and crisp. Do not over-fry.
  6. Serve immediately: Place the fried ice cream in a serving bowl, drizzle with chocolate syrup or honey, and top with whipped cream and a cherry if desired.
  7. Enjoy the crispy outside and cold, creamy inside — a perfect contrast of textures!
