poori with curry

Ingredients

  • 2 cups wheat flour (atta)
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon oil or ghee (for dough)
  • Water, as needed (about ½ cup)
  • Oil for deep frying
  • Optional: 1 tbsp semolina (sooji) for crispier pooris
  • Optional: ¼ tsp ajwain (carom seeds) for flavor

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix wheat flour and salt. Add 1 tsp oil and rub lightly into the flour.
  2. Gradually add water and knead to form a soft, smooth dough. Cover and rest for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls and roll each into a 3-4 inch circle.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan on medium-high heat. Test with a small piece of dough—it should rise quickly.
  5. Carefully slide each poori into hot oil. Press gently with a slotted spoon so it puffs up.
  6. Fry until golden brown on both sides. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  7. Serve hot with curry, halwa, or chutney.
