Namkeen

Ingredients

  • Besan (gram flour) – 2 cups
  • Rice flour – ½ cup
  • Salt – as per taste
  • Turmeric powder – ½ tsp
  • Red chili powder – 1 tsp
  • Carom seeds (ajwain) – ½ tsp
  • Oil – for deep frying
  • Flattened rice (poha) – 1 cup (optional)
  • Peanuts / roasted gram / cashews / curry leaves – for mixing

Steps

    1. Prepare the Dough or Batter

  1. In a large bowl, mix besan, rice flour, salt, turmeric, chili powder, and ajwain.
  2. Add a little water gradually to make a soft dough (for sev) or medium-thick batter (for boondi).

    3. 2. Heat Oil

  3. Pour oil into a deep frying pan or kadhai.
  4. Heat on medium flame until the oil is hot enough (test by dropping a small bit — it should rise immediately).

    5. 3. Fry the Sev or Boondi

  5. For Sev: Use a sev maker (chakli press with fine holes) and press the dough directly into hot oil.
  6. For Boondi: Pour batter through a perforated spoon to make small droplets.
  7. Fry till golden and crisp, then remove and place on tissue paper to absorb excess oil.

    8. 4. Roast or Fry Add-ons

  8. Fry or roast poha, peanuts, roasted gram, cashews, and curry leaves separately till crisp.
  9. Drain excess oil.

    10. 5. Mix and Season

  10. Combine all fried items in a large bowl.
  11. Sprinkle a little chaat masala or black salt for extra flavor.
  12. Mix well and let it cool completely.

    13. 6. Store

  13. Once cooled, store in an airtight container.
  14. It stays fresh and crunchy for 2–3 weeks.
