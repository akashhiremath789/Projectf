Parotta

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee (for dough)
  • Water, as needed (about ¾ cup)
  • Oil or ghee, for cooking and layering

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix maida, salt, and sugar. Add 2 tbsp oil and combine well.
  2. Gradually add water and knead into a soft, elastic dough. Knead for 8–10 minutes for best results.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and rest it for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into medium-sized balls. Roll one ball into a very thin sheet using oil to prevent sticking.
  5. Apply a little oil over the surface, then fold it in pleats (like a fan) and roll it into a spiral.
  6. Flatten gently and roll again into a thick round disc (do not press too hard).
  7. Heat a tawa on medium flame. Cook each parotta on both sides, applying oil or ghee, until golden brown and crisp.
  8. Once cooked, fluff the parottas by gently clapping them between your hands to separate the layers.
  9. Serve hot with kurma, salna, or chutney.
