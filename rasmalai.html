Rasmalai

Ingredients

  • 1 liter full-fat milk (for rasgullas)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice or vinegar (to curdle milk)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 liter full-fat milk (for rabri)
  • 1/4 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 10–12 saffron strands soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk
  • 2 tablespoons chopped pistachios and almonds

Steps

  1. Make Chenna (Paneer): Boil 1 liter of milk, then add lemon juice or vinegar gradually while stirring until it curdles. Strain through a muslin cloth and rinse with cold water. Hang for 30 minutes to remove excess water.
  2. Shape Rasgullas: Knead the chenna until smooth and soft. Divide into small equal balls and flatten slightly.
  3. Cook Rasgullas: In a wide pan, boil 4 cups water with 1 cup sugar. Add the chenna discs, cover, and cook on medium heat for 12–15 minutes. They will double in size. Remove and let them cool.
  4. Prepare Rabri (flavored milk): Boil 1 liter milk in a heavy-bottomed pan until it thickens to about half its quantity. Add 1/4 cup sugar, cardamom powder, and saffron milk. Mix well and simmer for 5–7 minutes.
  5. Assemble Rasmalai: Gently squeeze extra syrup from the cooked rasgullas and place them in warm rabri.
  6. Garnish with chopped pistachios and almonds. Chill for a few hours before serving.
  7. Serve cold and enjoy the rich, creamy Rasmalai!
