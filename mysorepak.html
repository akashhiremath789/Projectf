Mysore pak

Ingredients

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • A pinch of cardamom powder (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare ghee and pan: Grease a small square or rectangular tray with ghee and set aside. Keep the ghee warm in a separate pan.
  2. Roast the flour: In a heavy-bottomed pan, add 2 tablespoons of ghee and roast the gram flour on low flame until fragrant and slightly golden. This removes the raw smell. Set aside.
  3. Make sugar syrup: In another pan, combine sugar and water. Heat until the sugar dissolves and the syrup reaches a one-string consistency (when stretched between fingers, it forms a single thread).
  4. Add flour: Lower the flame and gradually add the roasted gram flour to the sugar syrup, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  5. Add ghee gradually: Slowly pour the warm ghee in small batches while stirring constantly. The mixture will start bubbling and leaving the sides of the pan as it thickens.
  6. Set the mixture: When the mixture becomes porous and light, immediately pour it into the greased tray. Level it quickly with a spatula.
  7. Cool and cut: Allow it to cool slightly, then cut into desired pieces (usually squares or diamonds) while still warm.
  8. Let it cool completely before serving. Enjoy the rich, melt-in-the-mouth Mysore Pak!
