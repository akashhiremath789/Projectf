Carrot Halwa

Ingredients

  • 1 kg carrots, grated
  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 3/4 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 4 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 10–12 cashews, chopped
  • 10–12 almonds, sliced
  • 10–12 raisins

Steps

  1. Heat ghee: In a heavy-bottomed pan or kadhai, heat 2 tablespoons of ghee. Add chopped cashews, almonds, and raisins. Fry until golden and remove them from the pan. Set aside.
  2. Cook carrots: In the same pan, add grated carrots and sauté for 4–5 minutes on medium heat until they slightly soften.
  3. Add milk: Pour in the milk and bring it to a boil. Lower the flame and simmer, stirring occasionally, until the milk reduces and thickens — about 30–40 minutes.
  4. Add sugar: Once most of the milk is absorbed, add sugar and mix well. The mixture will become a bit liquid again — continue cooking until it thickens.
  5. Add ghee and cardamom: Stir in the remaining ghee and cardamom powder. Cook until the halwa leaves the sides of the pan and becomes glossy.
  6. Garnish: Add the fried nuts and raisins. Mix well.
  7. Serve warm or chilled, as desired. Enjoy your delicious carrot halwa!
